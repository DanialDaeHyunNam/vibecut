About the "unverified developer" warning

The first time you open Vibecut, macOS or Windows will warn that it comes from an unverified developer. That message means the app isn't code-signed with a paid developer certificate — it is not a malware detection. Vibecut is fully open source: every line you run is public on GitHub, and you can build it from source yourself.

To open it: on macOS 15+ go to System Settings → Privacy & Security → Open Anyway (older macOS: right-click the app → Open). On Windows, click More info → Run anyway on the SmartScreen prompt.

Download only from the official GitHub Releases page. Vibecut is provided under the MIT license, as is.

One thing before you download

Vibecut is free and open source, but it isn't code-signed with a paid developer certificate yet — so your OS shows a warning on first launch. It's a missing-signature notice, not a malware detection. Here's exactly what you'll see, and what to do:

You'll see this on first open
"Vibecut" Not Opened

Apple could not verify "Vibecut" is free of malware that may harm your Mac or compromise your privacy.

Done Move to Trash
  1. Open Vibecut once and click Done (not Move to Trash).
  2. Go to System Settings → Privacy & Security, scroll down, click Open Anyway.
    On macOS 14 or older: just right-click Vibecut.app → Open → Open.
  3. That's it — the warning never comes back.
You'll see this when running the installer
Windows protected your PC

Microsoft Defender SmartScreen prevented an unrecognized app from starting. Running this app might put your PC at risk.

More info Don't run
  1. Click More info on the SmartScreen dialog.
  2. Click Run anyway — the button appears after step 1.
  3. That's it — Windows remembers your choice.
